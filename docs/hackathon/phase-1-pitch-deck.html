Study TUI · Hackathon Phase 1 · MVP

Studying isn't one chat with one PDF.

Material scattered across PDFs, scanned notes, textbooks, Calibre, and Zotero. Existing AI tools chat with one document and forget you when the tab closes — no quizzing, no weak-point tracking, no continuity.

Speaker notes · 01

Open with the audience's reality. Students and researchers don't have one neat document — they have a Calibre library, a Zotero collection, scanned handwritten notes, and downloaded textbooks scattered across folders. Existing AI tools chat with one document at a time and lose context when you close the tab. There is no progress tracking, no quizzing, no flashcards, no continuity. The problem isn't "I need to talk to a PDF." It's "I need a place to actually study from my materials over weeks."

Solution · the active study loop

A terminal-native study cockpit, not another chat-with-PDF app.

Load a real document. Ask grounded questions. Get quizzed on what you don't know. Turn misses into flashcards. Save notes, export, and come back later — your weak points remembered.

01 / Load

Bring your library

PDFs, image notes, OCR-aware. Calibre and Zotero plug in.

02 / Ask

Grounded chat

BM25 retrieval over loaded docs. Answers cite the page.

03 / Quiz

Weak-point hunt

Interactive quizzes target what you've actually missed.

04 / Return

Progress remembered

Misses become flashcards. Notes, summaries, exports persist per document.

study >load chapter 1 physics
study >what are the main ideas here?
study >quiz me on the weak points
study >make flashcards from the misses
study >how am i doing on this chapter?# picks up next session
Speaker notes · 02

Walk through the loop literally. Every command on screen is a real built command, not a roadmap promise. Emphasize that progress persists per-document via SQLite, so the system remembers your weak points the next time you open chapter 1. The differentiator vs. "chat with PDF" is the loop: ask → quiz → flashcard from miss → return. That's the whole pitch — the rest is execution.

This slide is the proof of life. Five integrated subsystems, all shipping in the MVP. Terminal UI on Textual + Rich. Document intelligence with PyMuPDF and EasyOCR including figure-aware PDF reading and BM25 retrieval. Provider-agnostic AI layer covering OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, Groq, Kimi, plus Ollama and llama.cpp for the privacy-conscious local-only path.
