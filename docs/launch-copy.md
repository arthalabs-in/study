## GitHub one-liner

Terminal-native AI study cockpit for people with real document libraries. Load local PDFs, Calibre books, or Zotero papers with natural language, then chat, quiz, make flashcards, export to Anki/PDF, and keep study progress across documents.

## Reddit

I built an open-source terminal study tool that works over existing local libraries instead of making you upload everything into another app.

You can point it at a documents folder, Calibre library, or Zotero setup, then do things like:
- `load chapter 1 physics`
- ask questions over the loaded doc
- generate quizzes and flashcards
- save notes and export to Anki or PDF
- keep progress tied to the document so review gets more personal over time

It supports local models and API providers, and the whole workflow stays keyboard-first.

Repo: https://github.com/arthalabs-in/study

## Hacker News

Show HN: Study TUI — terminal-native AI study cockpit for local PDFs, Calibre, and Zotero

Study TUI is an open-source terminal app for studying over document libraries you already have. Instead of just chatting with a PDF, it turns the loaded material into quizzes, flashcards, notes, exports, and persistent study progress.

The core loop is:
- load a document with natural language
- ask questions
- generate a quiz
- turn misses into flashcards
- export notes or Anki cards
- come back later and review with document-linked progress

Repo: https://github.com/arthalabs-in/study
